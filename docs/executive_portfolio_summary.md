# Malta Spa Commercial & Operational Management Portfolio

## Positioning
This portfolio is built as a Spa Manager commercial and operational decision system, not merely as a reporting dashboard.

It is designed to show how multi-outlet spa performance can be interpreted through commercial pressure, operating control, staffing realism, therapist structure, and management action discipline.

## Core Objective
The objective is to identify:
- which outlets are growth-ready
- which outlets are leaking commercial value
- which outlets are under team strain or burnout risk
- which operating conditions require management stabilization before commercial expansion

## Management Lenses
This portfolio is structured through four management lenses:
- commercial performance
- operational control
- treatment opportunity
- workforce realism

These lenses are used together so outlet performance is not judged on revenue alone.

## Executive Value
This portfolio demonstrates the ability to translate outlet-level signals into managerial action priorities across growth, control, and team sustainability.

It is intended to support decisions such as:
- where to protect premium performance
- where to tighten execution discipline
- where to stabilize staffing conditions
- where to pursue controlled commercial uplift

## Output Logic
The interpretation layer classifies outlet-month performance into:
- growth-ready
- leakage-risk
- team-strain / burnout-risk
- mixed / monitor

That logic is then translated into management-facing outputs such as:
- executive operating readouts
- outlet staffing management views
- therapist coaching summaries
- treatment opportunity summaries
- manager action queues

## Why It Matters
A spa business should not be managed on revenue alone. Growth that ignores operational strain can damage service quality, while weak execution can leak value even when demand exists.

This portfolio shows how a Spa Manager can decide where to grow, where to tighten execution, and where to stabilize the team first.

## Boundary Note
This project is presented as a management portfolio and decision-support model.

It is not presented as a live production system or as a direct internal export from one spa operator. External context is used to frame commercial conditions, while management interpretation remains anchored to structured operating logic and controlled portfolio assumptions.
