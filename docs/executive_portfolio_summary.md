# Malta Spa Management Portfolio

## Positioning
This portfolio is a management-oriented spa operations and decision-support portfolio for multi-outlet environments.

It is designed for reviewers, operators, and hiring managers who want to see how spa performance can be interpreted through commercial pressure, operating control, staffing realism, therapist structure, non-therapist operating-role design, and management action discipline.

It is not positioned as a live production deployment or a generic reporting dashboard.

## Core Objective
The objective is to identify:
- which outlets are growth-ready
- which outlets are leaking commercial value
- which outlets are under team strain or burnout risk
- which operating conditions require management stabilization before commercial expansion
- which teams need refresh training or coaching support
- which staff and therapists show reward-recognition potential under the modeled commercial framework

## Management Lenses
This portfolio is structured through six management lenses:
- commercial performance
- operational control
- treatment opportunity
- workforce realism
- operating-role coverage and execution discipline
- reward, coaching, and refresh-training governance

These lenses are used together so outlet performance is not judged on revenue alone.

## Executive Value
This portfolio demonstrates the ability to translate outlet-level signals into managerial action priorities across growth, control, team sustainability, recognition, and coaching.

It is intended to support decisions such as:
- where to protect premium performance
- where to tighten execution discipline
- where to stabilize staffing conditions
- where to pursue controlled commercial uplift
- where to reward strong selling or upsell behavior
- where to run refresh training before weak execution leaks more value

## Output Logic
The interpretation layer classifies outlet-month performance into:
- growth-ready
- leakage-risk
- team-strain / burnout-risk
- mixed / monitor

That logic is then translated into management-facing outputs such as:
- executive operating readouts
- outlet staffing management views
- therapist coaching summaries
- treatment opportunity summaries
- manager action queues
- staff commercial scoring layers
- therapist reward / top-bottom performance layers

## Operating-role extension
The latest management extension adds non-therapist operating-role architecture to the framework.

This includes structured baseline support logic for:
- spa receptionist
- spa attendant
- spa assistant manager
- spa manager

The purpose is to show that spa operating performance depends not only on therapist deployment, but also on front-desk continuity, room-readiness support, floor coordination, and management control coverage.

## Commercial contribution layer
The latest commercial extension adds role-aware selling and coaching logic.

This includes:
- retail-selling score across receptionist, spa attendant, assistant manager, and spa manager roles
- therapist upsell score combining treatment upsell, retail contribution, and service-quality guardrails
- reward-recognition eligibility signals
- bottom-group therapist detection for refresh training
- manager action queue integration for recognition, coaching, and commercial follow-up

This makes the portfolio more representative of real spa operations, where commercial discipline and service execution are shared responsibilities, not therapist-only outputs.

## Why It Matters
A spa business should not be managed on revenue alone. Growth that ignores operational strain can damage service quality, while weak execution can leak value even when demand exists.

A strong spa manager also needs to know:
- which commercial behaviors should be rewarded
- which service teams need coaching before performance slips further
- which operating roles support sustainable revenue instead of short-lived sales spikes

This portfolio shows how a Spa Manager can decide where to grow, where to tighten execution, where to stabilize the team first, and where to recognize or retrain people based on modeled evidence.

## Boundary Note
This project is presented as a management portfolio and decision-support model.

It is not presented as a live production deployment or as a direct internal export from one spa operator. External context is used to frame commercial conditions, while management interpretation remains anchored to structured operating logic, role-aware decision rules, and controlled portfolio assumptions.
