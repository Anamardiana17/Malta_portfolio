# 30/60/90-Day Management Application Plan

## Purpose
This document shows how the Malta_portfolio framework could be operationalized in a real spa management context over the first 90 days.

It is a recruiter-facing application layer built on top of the project’s governed intake, KPI visibility, business health interpretation, and decision-support structure.

It does not imply live deployment or the use of real company operating data.

## First 30 Days — Validate Context and Establish KPI Truth
Focus:
- understand the operating context across outlets
- validate baseline KPI truth and management reporting rhythm
- identify early leakage-risk, service-strain, and control weaknesses
- confirm what the portfolio indicates before action is scaled

Management application:
- review governed intake structure and available operating evidence
- establish baseline reading for revenue quality, utilization, yield, and strain signals
- use executive dashboard outputs to identify where business health differs across outlets
- separate operational truth from contextual demand support signals
- confirm immediate watchlist areas before introducing interventions

Expected management outcomes:
- baseline business-health view established
- initial outlet watchlist defined
- reporting cadence and review routine clarified
- early leakage / strain priorities identified

## Days 31–60 — Prioritize Interventions and Tighten Control
Focus:
- move from diagnosis into outlet-level action
- prioritize controllable operational interventions
- tighten staffing, pricing, promotion, and service-consistency discipline
- reduce avoidable leakage while protecting yield balance and therapist sustainability

Management application:
- use decision-support outputs to prioritize action queue items
- review staffing/control pressure alongside service consistency signals
- evaluate promotion and pricing choices against KPI guardrails
- focus outlet managers on the highest-value operational corrections first
- monitor whether corrective actions improve business health without creating new strain

Expected management outcomes:
- prioritized action plan activated
- outlet-specific control actions underway
- pricing/promotion discipline improved
- service and staffing issues reviewed with clearer evidence

## Days 61–90 — Embed Review Rhythm and Scale Decision Use
Focus:
- convert the framework into a recurring management rhythm
- track whether actions improve health under guardrails
- formalize routines for ongoing monitoring and escalation
- strengthen consistency of decision-making across outlets

Management application:
- embed regular executive review using governed outputs
- monitor trend movement and portfolio condition changes over time
- track which actions are reducing leakage, improving balance, or preventing escalation
- formalize how outlet-level issues move into portfolio-level management review
- refine management routines around KPI review, intervention follow-up, and operating discipline

Expected management outcomes:
- recurring review rhythm established
- action tracking tied to business-health movement
- stronger cross-outlet management consistency
- portfolio framework translated into practical operating use

## How This Connects to Malta_portfolio
This 30/60/90-day plan is supported by the existing repo structure:
- governed intake and controlled processing
- KPI / guardrail layer
- interpretation and business-health reading
- decision-support prioritization
- reviewer-facing GUI and supporting outputs

The purpose of this document is to show how the existing portfolio logic can be used in management practice, without changing the project’s core identity as a governed spa operations decision-support portfolio.
